Hill Climb racing isn't just about pressing the gas pedal—it’s about physics management. In ZyRace, the difference between a record time and a "Game Over" screen often comes down to millisecond decisions. Here are the top 5 strategies to master the tracks.

1. Mid-Air Rotation Control

When your car is in the air, you are not helpless. Use the Left and Right arrow keys to rotate your vehicle.

  • Lean Back: To prevent nose-diving upon landing.
  • Lean Forward: To grip steep upward slopes immediately upon contact.

2. Throttle Feathering

Holding down the gas key is a rookie mistake. On loose terrain or steep inclines, full throttle can cause wheel spin (loss of traction) or make the car flip backward. Feathering (tapping) the gas maintains traction and keeps the center of mass stable.

3. Momentum is Key

Some hills are too steep to climb from a standstill. You need to build momentum. If you are stuck at the bottom of a V-shape valley, reverse up the opposite side first to gather potential energy, then floor it to clear the obstacle.

4. Upgrade Suspension Before Engine

It's tempting to max out speed first, but a fast car is useless if it bounces uncontrollably.

Priority: Suspension > Tires > Engine > 4WD

A stable car allows you to keep the power down more often, resulting in faster overall times than a powerful car that crashes constantly.

5. Watch the Fuel Gauge

Speed runs are fun, but don't forget the fuel containers. Sometimes it is worth slowing down to grab a fuel can on an alternate path than rushing past it and running empty 100 meters later.
